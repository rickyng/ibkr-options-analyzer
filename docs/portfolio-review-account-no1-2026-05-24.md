#### Revised Analysis — Intentional Diversification Strategy

**Strategy Rationale (corrected):** These puts are designed to build Japanese equity positions at pre-selected entry prices — consistent with the Buffett/Graham approach of buying quality at a discount or collecting premium while waiting.

**What works:**

1. **Strike discipline matches existing cost basis.**

   - Sony puts at ¥3,100 (below avg cost ¥3,236) — lowering average cost

   - Itochu puts at ¥1,950-2,100 (near avg cost ¥2,020) — adding at similar price
   - Mitsubishi puts at ¥4,900-5,250 (near avg cost ¥5,294) — adding at similar price

2. **Quality Japanese businesses selected.**
   - **Nintendo (7974)**: Gaming moat, IP fortress, global brand
   - **Sony (6758)**: Diversified entertainment + sensors + gaming
   - **MUFG (8306)**: Largest Japanese bank, financial infrastructure
   - **Itochu (8001)**: Sogo shosha (trading house) — **Buffett owns Japanese trading companies via Berkshire**
   - **SoftBank (9984)**: Tech investment arm, quality underlying assets
   - **Nikkei ETF (1321)**: Broad index diversification

3. **Buffett precedent.** Berkshire invested $6B+ in five Japanese sogo shosha (Itochu, Mitsubishi, Mitsui, Sumitomo, Marubeni) starting 2020. The Itochu puts directly follow this playbook — buy quality Japanese businesses at attractive prices.

4. **Net positive P&L.** The JP put book is +¥46,420 (+$292) net. The OTM positions (14) far exceed the ITM positions (10) in count and aggregate P&L.

**Concerns:**

1. **7011.T (Mitsubishi Heavy Industries) — 7 puts with 5 ITM.**

4. **Notional sizing relative to portfolio.**

#### JP Put Strategy Verdict: SOUND STRATEGY, NEEDS SIZING DISCIPLINE

**The strategy is correct.** Using short puts to build Japanese positions is textbook Buffett/Graham — buy quality at a discount, or get paid to wait. The stock selection (Nintendo, Sony, Itochu, MUFG) follows Buffett's own Japan thesis.

**The risk is not the strategy — it's the sizing.**

If all ITM puts assign (worst case), you'd need ~$2-3M to buy stock, shifting the portfolio from 79% US Tech to potentially 40% US Tech / 40% JP. That may or may not be your intention.

**Recommendations:**
1. **Decide target JP allocation.** Is it 5%? 10%? 20%? Size puts to match.
2. **Mitsubishi Heavy is over-allocated.** 7 puts (5 ITM) = aggressive position building in one stock. Consider which strikes you actually want assignment on.
3. **Let the OTM puts expire.** Collect premium. Re-roll at similar strikes.
4. **Manage ITM puts before expiry.** For positions where you DON'T want assignment (¥6,000 Mitsubishi, ¥4,600 Mitsubishi Heavy), consider rolling or closing.
